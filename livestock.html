Program Overview

Growing food security
& rural incomes

For rural households in Kasese District, livestock and small-scale farming are among the most viable and culturally relevant pathways to economic independence. BARWODA's Livelihoods Program provides training, starter animals, and ongoing support to help members build sustainable farming enterprises.

With 90 members trained and international partners providing critical support, the program has created measurable improvements in household food security and income generation across the district.

"When a family has a reliable source of food and income from their own land and animals, they have a foundation for everything else — health, education, dignity."
— BARWODA Livelihoods Vision

Our Farming Programs

Three paths to
agricultural income

🐷
Pig Farming
Pig rearing is one of the most effective livestock programs for rural Uganda — pigs reproduce quickly, require limited space, and can be sold at local markets for significant household income. BARWODA provides training and starter pigs to members.
🐝
Beekeeping
Beekeeping offers a low-cost, high-return agricultural enterprise that also supports environmental conservation through pollination. Members are trained in hive management, honey harvesting, and the production of bee products for local markets.
🐓
Poultry Farming
Chicken and other poultry provide dual income streams through egg sales and meat production. Poultry farming is accessible to households with limited land and can be integrated with existing household food production systems.

Training Components

What our farmers learn

  • Animal husbandry and livestock health management
  • Feeding regimes and nutrition for pigs, poultry, and bees
  • Disease prevention and veterinary first aid
  • Hive construction and honey harvesting techniques
  • Market linkages and pricing strategies
  • Record keeping and basic financial management
  • Integration with household nutrition and food security
