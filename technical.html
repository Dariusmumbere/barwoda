Program Overview

Skills that unlock
economic freedom

BARWODA's Technical Education & Skills Development program is a cornerstone of economic empowerment for rural women in Kisinga Sub-county and the broader Kasese District. Since 1998, the program has equipped women with practical, income-generating skills.

Through training in typing and tailoring, beneficiaries gain the tools to become self-sufficient, start micro-businesses, and provide for their families — breaking the cycle of poverty one skill at a time.

"When a woman earns her own income, she gains the confidence to speak up, to invest in her children's future, and to shape her own destiny."
— BARWODA Founding Philosophy

Our Training Tracks

Two pathways to
independence

⌨️
Typing & Computer Skills
Hands-on training in typing equips women with office and administrative skills. Over 60 graduates are now self-employed, running typing services or securing employment in local businesses and institutions.
  • Practical typing speed and accuracy
  • Business document preparation
  • Office administration skills
  • Self-employment pathways
🪡
Tailoring & Garment Making
Comprehensive training in sewing and garment construction. 114 women have been trained, generating local income through small tailor shops, school uniform production, and community clothing services.
  • Machine and hand sewing techniques
  • Pattern cutting and garment design
  • Local uniform and clothing production
  • Micro-business skills integration
🌱
Business & Livelihood Integration
Skills training is paired with basic business literacy to ensure graduates can sustain their enterprises. Community networks enable women to support each other, share resources, and grow together.
  • Basic financial management
  • Peer learning networks
  • Market linkages in Kasese
  • VSLA savings group integration

Who We Serve

Built for the
most marginalized

The program specifically targets women who face the greatest barriers to economic participation — including those with limited formal education, young mothers, and women emerging from difficult circumstances.

  • Young women aged 18–35 with limited formal employment
  • Mothers seeking supplemental household income
  • Survivors of domestic violence rebuilding independence
  • Women with low literacy levels (training adapted accordingly)
  • Returnees from early marriages seeking new starts
