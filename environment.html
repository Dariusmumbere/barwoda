Program Overview

Planting the future,
tree by tree

Deforestation, soil erosion, and the effects of climate change are real and present threats to communities in Kasese District. BARWODA's Environmental Conservation Program responds with tangible, community-led reforestation and sustainable agriculture initiatives.

With over 9,500 trees planted and grafted mango farming introduced, BARWODA is creating a legacy of environmental stewardship while simultaneously generating income and nutrition for member households.

"The land that nourishes our communities today must be protected so it can nourish the generations who will come after us."
— BARWODA Environmental Vision

What We Do

Our environmental
initiatives

🌳
Community Tree Planting
Over 9,500 trees have been planted across Kasese District, with a primary focus in Kahokya Village. Tree planting events mobilize community members and reinforce a culture of environmental responsibility.
🥭
Grafted Mango Farming
BARWODA introduced grafted mango farming as a sustainable agroforestry practice that delivers dual benefits — generating household income from fruit sales while contributing to reforestation and soil stabilization.
🌾
Climate-Resilient Farming
Training members in climate-adaptive agricultural techniques — including water conservation, composting, and intercropping — to protect yields and food security in the face of changing rainfall patterns.

Why It Matters

The link between
nature and poverty

In rural Uganda, livelihoods depend directly on healthy land. Deforestation leads to soil erosion, reduced crop yields, lower water quality, and increased vulnerability to floods and drought — disproportionately affecting women who manage households and small farms.

  • Reforested land reduces flood risk and soil erosion
  • Shade trees improve water retention for crops
  • Fruit trees provide nutrition and market income
  • Women-led conservation builds community leadership
  • Expansion plans extend impact beyond Kahokya Village
