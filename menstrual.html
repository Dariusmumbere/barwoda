Program Overview

One solution,
two transformations

Menstrual health is one of the most overlooked barriers keeping girls out of school in rural Uganda. Without access to sanitary products, girls miss school days every month — falling behind academically and eventually dropping out. Meanwhile, women lack income-generating opportunities.

BARWODA's Menstrual Health Program addresses both challenges with a single, brilliant solution: training women to produce reusable, washable sanitary pads and distributing them to schoolgirls across Kasese District.

"A girl should never miss school because of her period. And a woman should always have the means to earn her own income."
— BARWODA Women's Livelihoods Program

💼
Economic Empowerment for Women
Women's groups are trained in sewing and tailoring specifically for pad production. The skills gained have economic value beyond the program — graduates can produce and sell pads as a sustainable small business, generating household income in an area of consistent, recurring demand.
  • Sewing and garment-making skills transfer
  • Micro-enterprise creation potential
  • Consistent local market demand
  • Integration with tailoring and skills programs
02
🎓
Menstrual Health for Girls
Pads produced by trained women are distributed to selected schoolgirls across Kasese District. Reusable pads are more sustainable, cheaper over time, and environmentally friendly compared to disposable alternatives — making them ideal for low-income rural contexts.
  • Girls stay in school every day of the month
  • Reduced dropout due to period stigma
  • Community sensitization on menstrual hygiene
  • Dignity and confidence for girls
What We Do

A complete program
from training to distribution

  • Train women groups in sewing and tailoring skills focused on pad production
  • Support production of high-quality reusable sanitary pads
  • Distribute pads to selected schoolgirls across Kasese District
  • Conduct community sensitization on menstrual hygiene management
  • Train girls and women on proper use, washing, and maintenance of reusable pads
  • Build awareness in schools, homes, and community spaces to reduce menstrual stigma
♻️
Sustainable by Design
Reusable pads last up to 12 months with proper care, making them far more cost-effective and environmentally sustainable than disposable alternatives in low-income communities.
🏫
School Retention Impact
Evidence shows girls with access to menstrual hygiene products miss significantly fewer school days, perform better academically, and are more likely to complete their education.
