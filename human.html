Program Overview

A community standing
up for its own rights

In Kasese District, harmful practices including domestic violence, Female Genital Mutilation (FGM), and Early Child Marriages (ECM) continue to deprive women and girls of their rights, health, and futures. BARWODA's Human Rights & GBV Prevention program confronts these challenges head-on.

Through a network of trained community volunteers, paralegal support, and strategic partnerships, BARWODA has built a powerful grassroots shield for the most vulnerable women and girls in the region.

"No woman should live in fear in her own home. No girl should be robbed of her childhood. These are not customs — they are violations."
— BARWODA Human Rights Charter

Issues We Address

Fighting the practices
that harm communities

🚫
Female Genital Mutilation
FGM remains practiced in parts of Kasese District, causing severe physical and psychological harm to girls. BARWODA trains community leaders and families to reject this harmful tradition and protect girls' bodies and futures.
🎓
Early Child Marriages
Girls married early lose access to education, economic independence, and full adulthood. BARWODA's volunteers work with families, schools, and local government to keep girls in school and delay marriage until adulthood.
🏠
Domestic Violence
Domestic violence is a leading cause of injury and death among women in rural Uganda. BARWODA sensitizes communities, trains survivors in their legal rights, and links victims to support services and paralegal assistance.
Our Approach

Four pillars of
community protection

  • Community Volunteer Network: 150+ trained volunteers serve as first-responders and sensitization agents in their villages
  • Paralegal Training: Supported by AWDF, members learn legal rights and procedures to assist survivors
  • Anti-FGM Campaign: 100 resourceful persons trained with WWDP-Germany Committee funding
  • Local Government Linkage: Collaboration with Kasese District structures for enforcement and survivor support
  • Community Sensitization: Regular awareness sessions in churches, schools, and markets
