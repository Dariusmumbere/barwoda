BARWODA is its people. 47 individuals — bound by conviction, community, and the belief that rural women deserve better.

How It Started

Five women who
refused to accept

In 1998, five women in Kisinga Sub-county looked around them and saw poverty, exploitation, and inequality. They did not look away. Instead, they organized — forming what would become BARWODA.

Their names are not widely known outside their community, but their decision to act changed hundreds of lives. Today, BARWODA's 47 members carry that founding spirit forward — each one committed to the vision those five women planted in 1998.

How We're Organized

Roles & structure

Executive Committee
Elected from within the membership, provides strategic direction, financial oversight, and organizational leadership.
Program Teams
Members organized by program area — literacy, livelihoods, human rights, health, and environment — deliver BARWODA's work on the ground.
Community Volunteers
150+ trained volunteers extend BARWODA's reach beyond formal membership — delivering critical services in surrounding villages.
Member Contributions

What our members do

✓
Deliver Literacy Training

Community teachers from the membership deliver functional literacy classes to adults across Kasese.

✓
Run Skills Programs

Member trainers teach typing, tailoring, and reusable sanitary pad production — generating economic independence.

✓
Advocate for Human Rights

Trained member volunteers sensitize communities on domestic violence, FGM, and early child marriage.

✓
Manage Livestock Programs

90 members directly participate in pig farming, beekeeping, and poultry projects supported by international partners.

✓
Plant Trees & Conserve Land

Members lead environmental conservation — planting trees and promoting climate-resilient agriculture.

BARWODA members in action — building community, building futures. Every program is delivered by people who live and work within the communities they serve.
