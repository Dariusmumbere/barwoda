How It Started

Five women who
refused to accept

In 1998, five women in Kisinga Sub-county looked around them and saw poverty, exploitation, and inequality. They did not look away. Instead, they organized — forming what would become BARWODA.

Their names are not widely known outside their community, but their decision to act changed hundreds of lives. They are the reason 145+ people can read and write. They are the reason 95 children stayed in school. They are the reason 9,500+ trees stand where there was bare land.

Today, BARWODA's 47 members carry that founding spirit forward — each one committed to the vision those five women planted in 1998.

How We're Organized

Roles & structure

BARWODA operates as a member-driven organization. Leadership emerges from within the community, and every member plays an active role in shaping the direction of the organization.

🏛️
Executive Committee
Elected from within the membership, the executive committee provides strategic direction, financial oversight, and organizational leadership for BARWODA's programs and initiatives.
📋
Program Teams
Members organized by program area — literacy, livelihoods, human rights, health, and environment — deliver BARWODA's work on the ground across Kasese District communities.
🤝
Community Volunteers
150+ trained volunteers extend BARWODA's reach beyond its formal membership — delivering human rights education, literacy support, and health programs in surrounding villages.
Member Contributions

What our members do

✓
Deliver Literacy Training

Community volunteer teachers from within the membership deliver functional literacy classes to adults across Kasese.

✓
Run Skills Programs

Member trainers teach typing, tailoring, and reusable sanitary pad production — generating real economic independence.

✓
Advocate for Human Rights

Trained member volunteers sensitize communities on domestic violence, FGM, and early child marriage — protecting the most vulnerable.

✓
Manage Livestock Programs

90 members directly participate in pig farming, beekeeping, and poultry projects supported by international partners.

✓
Plant Trees & Conserve Land

Members lead environmental conservation efforts — planting trees, promoting climate-resilient agriculture, and protecting natural resources.

✓
Support Vulnerable Children

Members mobilize resources and advocate to ensure 95 vulnerable children receive school fees, materials, and clothing.

BARWODA members in action — building community, building futures. Every program is delivered by people who live and work within the communities they serve.
