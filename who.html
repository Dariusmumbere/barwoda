About BARWODA

Who We Are

A grassroots organization born from the determination of five women — growing into a movement that transforms rural communities across Western Uganda.

1998Year
Founded
Our Origin

Born from necessity,
built on courage

Bayira Rural Women's Development Association — BARWODA — was founded in 1998 by five visionary women in Kisinga Sub-county, Bukonzo, Kasese District, Western Uganda.

These founding women saw the reality around them clearly: widespread poverty, gender inequality, and the systemic exploitation of rural women. Rather than accept these conditions, they organized. They built something new — a community of women committed to lifting one another up.

Over 26 years later, BARWODA has grown into a 47-member organization (45 women, 2 men), formally registered with the Kasese District Local Government – Community Development Department. We are proudly non-political, non-religious, and non-profit.

How We Work

Our philosophy

BARWODA believes in community-driven development. We treat communities as partners — never as beneficiaries. Lasting change comes from within.

01
Knowledge & Capacity Building

Empowering individuals through education, skills, and confidence.

02
Partnership, Not Charity

Working with communities as equal partners, respecting agency.

03
Dignity & Human Rights

Promoting equality, protecting rights, ending violence.

04
Inclusive Development

Prioritizing the most vulnerable — leaving no one behind.

We are committed to creating a just, inclusive, and humane society, where individuals have the power to shape their own future.
BARWODA Core Philosophy
Our Approach

Every BARWODA program builds lasting local capacity — not dependency. We train teachers from within communities, empower local leaders, and ensure skills remain long after projects end.

Why We Exist

Our core objectives

💡
Eliminate Poverty & Illiteracy
Promote women-led initiatives to eliminate poverty, unemployment, hunger, disease, and illiteracy in rural Kasese.
🤲
Strengthen Marginalized Voices
Strengthen participation of rural women in development programs and decision-making.
🌿
Environmental Sustainability
Advance conservation, plant trees, promote climate-resilient farming, protect natural resources.
⚖️
Address Gender Inequality
Tackle inequality, reduce feminization of poverty through education and economic empowerment.
