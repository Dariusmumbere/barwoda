About BARWODA

Who We Are

A grassroots organization born from the determination of five women — growing into a movement that transforms rural communities across Western Uganda.

1998Year
Founded
Our Origin

Born from necessity,
built on courage

Bayira Rural Women's Development Association — BARWODA — was founded in 1998 by five visionary women in Kisinga Sub-county, Bukonzo, Kasese District, Western Uganda.

These founding women saw the reality around them clearly: widespread poverty, gender inequality, and the systemic exploitation of rural women. Rather than accept these conditions, they organized. They built something new — a community of women committed to lifting one another up.

Over 26 years later, BARWODA has grown into a 47-member organization (45 women, 2 men), formally registered with the Kasese District Local Government – Community Development Department. We are proudly non-political, non-religious, and non-profit.

How We Work

Our philosophy

BARWODA believes in community-driven development. We treat communities as partners — never as beneficiaries. We believe that lasting change comes from within.

01
Knowledge & Capacity Building

Empowering individuals through education, skills, and the confidence to shape their own futures.

02
Partnership, Not Charity

Working with communities as equal partners, respecting their agency, knowledge, and leadership.

03
Dignity & Human Rights

Promoting equality, protecting rights, and building a society free from violence and discrimination.

04
Inclusive Development

Prioritizing the most vulnerable — especially women and children — leaving no one behind.

We are committed to creating a just, inclusive, and humane society, where individuals have the power to shape their own future.
BARWODA Core Philosophy
Our Approach

Every program BARWODA runs is designed to build lasting local capacity — not dependency. We train teachers from within communities, empower local leaders, and ensure that the skills and knowledge we share remain long after any project ends.

Why We Exist

Our core objectives

💡
Eliminate Poverty & Illiteracy
Promote women-led initiatives to eliminate poverty, unemployment, hunger, disease, and illiteracy in rural communities across Kasese District.
🤲
Strengthen Marginalized Voices
Strengthen the participation of marginalized rural women in development programs and decision-making processes at all levels.
🌿
Environmental Sustainability
Advance environmental conservation and sustainable development practices — planting trees, promoting climate-resilient farming, and protecting natural resources.
⚖️
Address Gender Inequality
Tackle gender inequality head-on and reduce the feminization of poverty through education, advocacy, and economic empowerment programs.
