Program Overview

The power of
reading and writing

BARWODA's Adult Literacy Program addresses one of the most fundamental barriers to women's empowerment — illiteracy. In rural Kasese District, many women were denied formal education in their youth, leaving them unable to read contracts, health information, or communicate in writing.

Since its launch, the program has enabled over 145 adults — primarily women — to read and write, transforming their daily lives, economic opportunities, and participation in community decision-making.

"Literacy is not just about reading words — it is about reading the world and claiming your rightful place in it."

How It Works

A model built on
community trust

The Adult Literacy Program uses a community-volunteer model — training educated community members to become literacy facilitators within their own villages, making learning accessible, trusted, and culturally relevant.

01
Community Mobilization
BARWODA engages village leaders and community structures to identify adult learners and recruit volunteer teachers from the local area.
02
Volunteer Teacher Training
Selected volunteers receive training in adult functional literacy pedagogy — ensuring lessons are practical, respectful, and relevant to rural daily life.
03
Flexible Class Sessions
Classes are held at times that accommodate farming and domestic schedules, reducing barriers to attendance for women with household responsibilities.
04
Functional Outcomes
Graduates can read health information, sign documents, write letters, and participate fully in financial literacy and business training programs.
What We Teach

Functional skills for
real life

  • Reading and writing in local language and English
  • Basic numeracy and arithmetic
  • Reading health and government documents
  • Writing personal letters and forms
  • Financial literacy fundamentals
  • Civic literacy and rights awareness
  • Integration with skills training programs
🔗
Linked to Economic Programs
Literacy graduates are encouraged to join BARWODA's typing, tailoring, and livelihood programs — creating a seamless pathway from literacy to economic independence.
👩🏫
Community Volunteer Teachers
Local educated community members are trained and deployed as literacy facilitators. This model builds local capacity, creates trust, and ensures cultural sensitivity in learning.
📖
Adult-Centred Curriculum
Unlike children's education, adult literacy at BARWODA uses practical, experience-based learning rooted in participants' daily realities — farming, family, health, and commerce.
🌍
Community Transformation
When a mother can read, her children are more likely to stay in school. Literacy has a generational ripple effect — each newly literate adult enriches the whole community.
