Who We Are

Built by women,
for everyone

BARWODA — Bayira Rural Women's Development Association — is a non-political, non-religious, non-profit organization officially registered with the Kasese District Local Government with 47 members (45 women, 2 men).

Our VisionA knowledgeable, skilled, and empowered women’s community in Kasese District that actively participates in decision-making and drives sustainable development.
Our GoalTo unite and engage rural women in initiatives that improve their standards of living while fostering a community free from violence, especially domestic violence, for sustainable development.
Our PhilosophyCommunity-driven, dignity-first, rights-based approach
Our MethodCapacity building, partnership, and inclusion

Our Journey

Milestones that
define us

1998
BARWODA Founded
Five visionary women establish the organization in Kisinga Sub-county, Kasese District, to combat poverty, gender inequality, and exploitation of rural women.
Early 2000s
Literacy & Skills Programs Launch
Adult literacy training begins with community volunteers. Technical education in typing and tailoring delivers early economic independence to dozens of women.
Mid 2000s
Human Rights Network Established
50+ community volunteers trained in human rights advocacy. Paralegal training supported by the African Women's Development Fund (AWDF).
2010s
International Partnerships Formed
Partnerships with Marrie-Schlei Verein (Germany), Oxted One World Group (UK), and 1% Fund (New York) catalyze livestock projects reaching 90 farming members.
Recent
MaliMali Medical Centre & Environmental Expansion
Community health facility established in Kyarumba providing holistic care. Over 9,500 trees planted and grafted mango farming introduced for food security and income.
Today
Growing Impact, Brighter Futures
47 members, 145+ people made literate, 95 children supported, 150 human rights volunteers active — and growing. BARWODA continues empowering Kasese District communities.
