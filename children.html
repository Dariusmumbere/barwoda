Program Overview

No child left
behind

In Kasese District, thousands of children face circumstances that threaten their access to education and basic dignities — poverty, parental death or illness, domestic violence, and displacement. BARWODA's Children in Difficult Circumstances (CDC) program provides a safety net for the most vulnerable.

By supporting 95 children with school fees, scholastic materials, clothing, and psychosocial care, BARWODA ensures that poverty does not become destiny — and that every child has a chance to learn, grow, and flourish.

"A child who goes to school fed, clothed, and carrying books can imagine a future. Our job is to make sure that future is possible for every single child we can reach."
— BARWODA Child Welfare Mandate

What We Provide

Three pillars of
child support

🏫
School Fees
BARWODA pays school fees for vulnerable children attending Universal Primary Education (UPE) schools, removing the financial barrier that keeps countless children at home and out of classrooms across Kasese District.
📦
Scholastic Materials
Every supported child receives the books, pencils, pens, exercise books, and other scholastic materials they need to participate fully in school — because showing up is only the beginning.
👕
Clothing
Appropriate clothing — including school uniforms where required — is provided so children can attend school with dignity, comfort, and the confidence that comes from being properly dressed among their peers.
Who We Reach

The children who
need us most

BARWODA's CDC program focuses on children facing the most severe barriers to education and basic welfare. Beneficiaries are identified in partnership with community leaders, local government structures, and school headteachers.

  • Orphans and children who have lost one or both parents
  • Children from households experiencing extreme poverty
  • Children of survivors of domestic violence
  • Children at risk of early marriage or child labour
  • Children with disabilities facing additional barriers
  • Street children and those lacking stable housing
Psychosocial Support

Healing hearts,
not just providing things

Material support is necessary but not sufficient. Many CDC program children carry the weight of trauma, grief, and instability. BARWODA provides psychosocial support — creating safe spaces where children can process their experiences and build resilience.

🧠
Psychosocial Care
Trained community health workers and BARWODA members provide structured psychosocial support for children experiencing trauma — coordinated through the MaliMali Medical Centre and community networks.
