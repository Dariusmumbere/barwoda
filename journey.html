Founding Era
1998
BARWODA is Founded
Five visionary women in Kisinga Sub-county, Bukonzo, Kasese District come together with a shared conviction: the poverty and exploitation surrounding them is not inevitable. They establish the Bayira Rural Women's Development Association — BARWODA — a community-based organization with no external funding and nothing but determination.

"We saw the poverty, the inequality, the suffering of women around us. We refused to accept it as normal. We organized."

FoundingKaseseWomen's Rights
1998
Official Registration
BARWODA is formally registered with the Kasese District Local Government – Community Development Department, establishing its legal status as a non-political, non-religious, non-profit organization. This registration gives the organization credibility to mobilize resources and partner with institutions.

Early Growth
Early 2000s
Adult Literacy Program Launches
BARWODA begins delivering functional literacy training through community volunteer teachers. Women and men who had never learned to read or write gain the foundational skills needed to participate in economic and civic life. The program is built from within — using local talent to teach local people.
EducationLiteracyCommunity
Early 2000s
Technical Education — Typing & Tailoring
BARWODA launches technical education programs in typing and tailoring — two skills with immediate economic application. Dozens of women train, and many achieve self-employment, generating income from within their communities. Over time, 60+ women become self-employed through typing and 114 train in tailoring.
VocationalEconomic Empowerment
Deepening Roots
Mid 2000s
Human Rights Volunteer Network
BARWODA trains over 50 community volunteers in human rights advocacy — equipping them to identify, document, and respond to cases of domestic violence, early child marriage (ECM), and Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) within their communities. A grassroots human rights infrastructure takes root in Kasese.
Human RightsAnti-FGMGBV
Mid 2000s
AWDF Paralegal Training Partnership
The African Women's Development Fund (AWDF) partners with BARWODA to deliver paralegal training, strengthening the legal literacy and advocacy capacity of community volunteers. This partnership marks BARWODA's entry into the international development funding landscape.

This partnership with AWDF transformed our ability to help women navigate legal challenges around land rights, domestic violence, and inheritance.

PartnershipAWDFLegal Rights
International Partnerships
2010s
Livestock Projects with Global Partners
With funding from Marrie-Schlei Verein (Germany), Oxted One World Group (UK), 1% Fund (New York), and International Christian Service for Africa, BARWODA launches pig farming, beekeeping, and poultry programs. 90 members are trained and supported, building sustainable household income across Kasese.
LivestockGermanyUKUSA
2010s
Anti-FGM & ECM Campaign — WWDP Germany
With funding from the WWDP-Germany Committee, BARWODA trains 100 resourceful persons to fight Female Genital Mutilation and Early Child Marriage. The campaign reaches thousands of community members across Kasese, shifting cultural norms and protecting girls and women from harmful practices.
Anti-FGMWWDPChild Protection
2010s
Children in Difficult Circumstances Program
BARWODA begins supporting 95 vulnerable children with school fees for UPE schools, scholastic materials, and clothing. Many of these children come from families affected by poverty, HIV/AIDS, or domestic violence — and BARWODA ensures they remain in school.
ChildrenEducationCDC
Expansion
Recent
MaliMali Medical Centre Opens
BARWODA establishes the MaliMali Medical Centre in Kyarumba — a community health facility providing outpatient and inpatient services, dental care, and eye care. The centre offers holistic care to communities that previously had limited access to healthcare, and supports the physical, mental, and spiritual wellbeing of patients.
HealthcareMaliMaliKyarumba
Recent
9,500+ Trees Planted
BARWODA's environmental conservation program reaches a major milestone — over 9,500 trees planted across Kahokya Village and surrounding areas. Grafted mango farming is introduced, providing both food security and income for farming households. Climate-resilient agricultural practices are promoted across the district.
EnvironmentClimateTrees
Recent
Menstrual Health & Sanitary Pad Production
BARWODA trains women's groups to produce reusable sanitary pads, addressing both economic empowerment and girls' menstrual health simultaneously. Pads are distributed to selected schoolgirls across Kasese District, reducing school absenteeism and supporting girls' education.

Growing Impact, Brighter Futures
BARWODA today has 47 members, partnerships spanning four continents, and a track record of transformative impact: 145+ people made literate, 114 women trained in tailoring, 95 children supported, 150 human rights volunteers active, 9,500+ trees planted, and a functioning community medical centre.

Twenty-six years after five women decided enough was enough, BARWODA continues to grow — and the story is far from over.
